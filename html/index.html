WHAT IS THE OBJECTIVE OF THE PROJECT?

The main objective is to train future professionals who are studying Degrees of Vocational Training in the area of ​​sustainability, taking as a point of reference the calculation and reduction of the carbon footprint.

WHAT ARE THE SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES OF THE PROJECT?

There are 7 main goals:
• Train students and teachers in calculating the carbon footprint.
• Reduce CO2 emissions from academic centers by 30%.
• Inculcate 'green skills' and make the academic environment aware, both teachers and students, of the concept of climate change (its causes, consequences and actions we can take to help stop it).
• Exchange of good practices between educational centers (especially between students) internationally.
• Social and gender inclusion involving 100% of the students, with and without special needs.
• Involve all partners and related companies in the most important activities important on calculation and reduction of carbon coal.
• Design new environmental policies and sustainability plans in the centers academics

HOW LONG DOES THE PROJECT LAST?

The project has an initial duration of 2 years and 3 months: September 2020- December 2022. Due to the pandemic, an extension has been requested and the project will end in April 2023.

HOW MANY PARTNERS PARTICIPATE?

The project is directed by the Cuatrovientos Integrated Center and is also made up of educational centres: CENIFER (Higher Integrated Center for Renewable Energies), Technologische Fachoberschule Bozen, HTBLA Wolfsberg, Göranssonska Skolan and the consultancy STENUM Unternehmensberatung und Forschungsgesellschaft fur Umweltfragen MBH

WHERE ARE THE PARTICIPATING PARTNERS FROM?

2 Spanish partners: CI CUATROVIENTOS AND CENIFER, based in Pamplona (Navarra). 1 Italian partner: Technologische Fachoberschule Bozen, based in Bozen (Alto Adige – Tyrol South) 2 Austrian partners: HTBLA Wolfsberg, based in Wolfsberg (Kärnten) and STENUM, based in Graz (Steiermark) 1 Swedish partner: Göranssonska Skolan, based in Sandviken (Gävleborgs län)

HOW MANY ACTIVITIES HAVE BEEN CARRIED OUT?

Each partner has developed different activities. Some examples are: conferences awareness, studies of energy efficiency in educational centers, installation of solar panels, tree planting, calculator to calculate the CO2 consumption of the centers, mobile application for calculating digital CO2 consumption at the academic level, meetings with specialized companies

ARE THE SDGS WORKED?

Yes, CF Train in VET works on the SDGs:
• 7 - Guarantee access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for everyone
• 11 – Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable
• 12 – Ensuring sustainable consumption and production patterns
• 13 – Adopt urgent measures to combat climate change and its effects
• 17 – Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the Global Partnership for Sustainable development
